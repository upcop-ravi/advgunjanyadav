  2. Investigation

    Police gather evidence (90 Days). We monitor fair investigation.

Q. How can I get Anticipatory Bail in Ghaziabad?

You can file for Anticipatory Bail under Section 482 of BNSS (formerly Sec 438 CrPC) in the District Court or Lucknow High Court before arrest. We specialize in securing pre-arrest bail for false FIRs and matrimonial disputes.

Q. What to do if police refuse to register my FIR?

If police refuse your complaint, we can file an application under Section 175(3) BNSS (previously 156(3) CrPC) directly before the Magistrate to court-order the registration of your FIR immediately.

Q. Can a criminal case be quashed?

Yes, the Allahabad High Court (Lucknow Bench) has inherent powers under Section 528 BNSS (Sec 482 CrPC) to quash FIRs that are frivolous, false, or result from a compromise between parties.

Q. Do you handle NDPS and specialized act cases?

Absolutely. We have extensive experience in NDPS (Narcotics), PMLA, and Gangster Act bail matters, often involving complex technical evidence and procedural non-compliance defense strategies.

Common Questions
on Criminal Defense

How can I get Anticipatory Bail if I fear arrest?
You can file for Anticipatory Bail under Section 482 of BNSS (formerly Sec 438 CrPC) in the Sessions Court or High Court. This prevents police from arresting you while the investigation continues. Immediate legal action is crucial to avoid custody.
What legal remedy do I have if Police refuse to file an FIR?
If the Station House Officer (SHO) refuses to register your FIR, you can approach the Superintendent of Police (SP). If that fails, we can file an application under Section 175(3) of BNSS (formerly 156(3) CrPC) before the Magistrate to court-order the registration of the FIR.
Can a false criminal case (498A, SC/ST Act) be quashed?
Yes. The High Court has inherent powers under Section 528 of BNSS (formerly Sec 482 CrPC) to quash FIRs that are frivolous, malicious, or filed solely to harass. We specialize in quashing proceedlings at the Lucknow High Court.
What is the procedure for Bail in NDPS (Drug) cases?
NDPS bail is complex due to strict conditions under Section 37. We focus on technical procedural lapses by the police (search & seizure violations) to secure bail. It requires specialized knowledge of the NDPS Act and recent Supreme Court judgments.
Do I have to appear in court for every hearing?
Not necessarily. We can file a Section 355 BNSS (formerly 205 CrPC) application to dispense with your personal attendance for routine hearings, allowing your advocate to represent you while you continue your daily life.
How long does a Criminal Trial take in Ghaziabad Court?
Trials can vary from 1-3 years depending on evidence and witnesses. However, we relentlessly push for speedy trials under Article 21 rights and ensure no unnecessary adjournments delay your justice.
