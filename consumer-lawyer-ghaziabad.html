Who Can File a Complaint?

Under Section 2(5) of the Consumer Protection Act, 2019, the following entities are legally empowered to seek redressal for any deficiency in services or defective goods.

A Consumer

Any individual who has bought goods or hired services for personal use (not for resale or commercial purpose).

Consumer Association

Registered voluntary consumer bodies can file a complaint on behalf of a single consumer or a group.

The Government

The Central Government or any State Government can initiate legal action for broader public interest.

Legal Heir / Guardian

Family members or legal guardians can file on behalf of a deceased consumer or a minor.

Jurisdiction & Forum

The Consumer Protection Act, 2019 establishes a three-tier quasi-judicial mechanism for redressal of consumer disputes. The pecuniary jurisdiction determines where your complaint must be filed.

01

District Commission

Up to ?50 Lakhs

The first court of instance for claims where the value of goods or services paid does not exceed ?50 Lakhs. Located in each district (e.g., Ghaziabad/Ghaziabad).

02

State Commission

?50 Lakhs - ?2 Crores

Located in the state capital (Lucknow). Handles original complaints between ?50 Lakhs and ?2 Crores, and appeals against District Commission orders.

03

National Commission

Above ?2 Crores

The apex body (NCDRC) in New Delhi. Handles original complaints exceeding ?2 Crores and appeals against State Commission orders.

Common Litigant Mistakes

Limitation Period Expired

A consumer complaint must be filed within 2 years from the date of the cause of action. Filing late without sufficient cause leads to immediate dismissal.

Wrong Jurisdiction

Filing in a court that lacks Pecuniary (value-based) or Territorial (location-based) jurisdiction will result in the case being returned.

Incomplete Evidence

Relying on oral assurances instead of documentary proof (bills, emails, job sheets). Courts decide strictly based on the records placed on file.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I file a complaint for a 2-year-old purchase?

Yes, the limitation period for filing a consumer complaint is generally 2 years from the date on which the cause of action arose. However, delays can be condoned by the Commission if sufficient cause is shown.

What qualifies as "Medical Negligence"?

Medical negligence occurs when a healthcare provider fails to provide the standard of care that a reasonably competent professional would have provided under similar circumstances, resulting in injury or death.

Is a lawyer mandatory in Consumer Court?

While the law allows consumers to represent themselves, engaging a specialized advocate is highly recommended for complex matters involving Insurance Repudiation, Real Estate, or Corporate Liability to ensure technical arguments are legally sound.

Can I sue an E-commerce portal for a fake product?

Absolutely. Under the CPA 2019, e-commerce platforms are liable as "Product Sellers" if they fail to provide details of the manufacturer or have significant control over the transaction.
