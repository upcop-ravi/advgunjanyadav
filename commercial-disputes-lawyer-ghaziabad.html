SPEEDY JUSTICE

Timeline of Litigation

We strictly adhere to the mandatory timelines of the Commercial Courts Act, 2015 to ensure rapid dispute resolution.

STEP 01

Pre-Institution Mediation

Section 12A

Mandatory exhaustion of remedy via mediation before filing suit, fostering amicable settlement. Essential pre-requisite unless urgent interim relief is sought.

STEP 02

Case Management Hearing

Order XV-A

Strict judicial oversight to frame non-negotiable timelines, limit adjournments, and fast-track the trial process immediately after pleadings.

STEP 03

Summary Judgment

Order XIII-A

Empowers courts to decide claims without a full trial if the defense lacks a real prospect of success, ensuring swift disposal of meritless disputes.

STEP 04

Final Judgment

Time-Bound Decree

Mandatory pronouncement of judgment within 90 days from the conclusion of arguments, guaranteeing a definitive legal outcome for businesses.

Commercial Disputes – Common Mistakes to Avoid

Ignoring Arbitration Clauses

Filing a suit directly when an arbitration clause exists can lead to immediate dismissal under Section 8 of the Arbitration Act.

Missing Limitation Periods

Commercial suits typically have a strict 3-year limitation. Delaying legal action can permanently bar your right to recover dues.

Inadequate Documentation

Failing to maintain signed invoices, delivery challans, or email confirmations weakens the evidence for your claim significantly.

Ignoring Legal Notices

Not replying to a demand notice (especially under IBC or MSME Act) can be treated as an admission of debt or liability.

Relying on Verbal Pacts

"Gentleman's agreements" are inadmissible. Always ensure commercial terms and amendments are documented in writing.

Wrong Forum Selection

Filing a commercial suit in a regular Civil Court instead of a designated Commercial Court delays the process and may lack jurisdiction.

Common Queries

Frequently Asked
Questions

What qualifies as a "Commercial Dispute"?
Commercial disputes include conflicts related to business contracts, export-import, franchising, distribution agreements, intellectual property, maritime law, and shareholder agreements. generally, any suit involving a commercial transaction above specified value falls under this Act.
Is Mediation mandatory before filing a suit?
Yes. Under Section 12A of the Commercial Courts Act, 2015, Pre-Institution Mediation (PIM) is mandatory unless you urgently require interim relief (like an injunction or stay order) from the Court.
How long does a commercial case take?
The Commercial Courts Act mandates strictly time-bound proceedings. Case Management Hearings are held to set timelines, and judgments must be pronounced within 90 days of concluding arguments.
What is Summary Judgment?
Summary Judgment (Order XIII-A) allows the court to decide a claim without a full trial or recording oral evidence if the other party has no real prospect of success. It is a powerful tool for swift disposal.
Can MSMEs get faster recovery?
Absolutely. Registered MSMEs are entitled to compound interest at 3 times the bank rate for delayed payments under the MSME Development Act, 2006. These disputes can also be referred to MSME Facilitation Councils.
Is a written contract necessary?
While a written contract is ideal, lawsuits can still be filed based on invoices, purchase orders, email delivery confirmations, and WhatsApp admissions. Digital evidence is fully admissible under Section 65B of the Evidence Act.
Do you handle Arbitration matters?
Yes. We assist in the appointment of Arbitrators (Section 11), seeking interim protection (Section 9), and challenging or enforcing Arbitral Awards (Section 34 & 36) before the appropriate courts in Ghaziabad and Lucknow.
What is the cost of filing a commercial suit?
Court fees are ad-valorem (percentage of claim value) as per state laws. Legal fees depend on the complexity of the matter. We offer a transparent fee structure compliant with Bar Council standards.
